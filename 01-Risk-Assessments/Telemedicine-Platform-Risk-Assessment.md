# Telemedicine Platform Risk Assessment

## Executive Summary

This risk assessment evaluates the cybersecurity risks associated with the MyDoc247 Telemedicine Platform, a digital healthcare organization providing telemedicine consultations, electronic medical records (EMR), and digital prescription services.

The objective of this assessment is to identify potential threats, vulnerabilities, and operational risks that may impact the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of healthcare information and services.

Key risks identified include phishing attacks, unauthorized access to patient records, ransomware incidents, third-party service disruptions, and weak identity management controls.

Recommended mitigation measures include Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA), employee security awareness training, endpoint protection, regular vulnerability assessments, and enhanced vendor risk management processes.

## Scope

Recommended Controls
## Recommended Controls

### Access Management

- Enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- Conduct quarterly access reviews
- Apply least-privilege access principles

### Security Awareness

- Conduct annual security awareness training
- Run phishing simulation exercises
- Establish incident reporting procedures

### Endpoint Security

- Deploy endpoint detection and response (EDR)
- Implement patch management processes
- Enforce device encryption

### Data Protection

- Encrypt data at rest and in transit
- Implement regular backups
- Apply data retention policies

### Vendor Management

- Perform annual vendor risk assessments
- Review third-party security certifications
- Include security requirements in contracts

Conclusion
## Conclusion
